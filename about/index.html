About

I am a college dropout in my mid-twenties. Well, my late-ish mid-twenties, but closer to my mid-twenties than my late-twenties. Screw it. I’m 27. I was diagnosed with ADD when I was a freshman in high school and went through a number of different medications and incorrect diagnoses over the next decade or so. I spent about six years in college accomplishing three years of a degree in computer engineering. After “taking a break” from college, I found a psychologist who was willing to start from scratch to actually figure out what was going on instead of just giving me pills and sending me out the door. It turns out I don’t have ADD, depression, or bipolar disorder (all of which I was “diagnosed” with at one point or another). I have Generalized Anxiety Disorder which leads me to distraction in a way that is similar to ADHD, but for different reasons and with a different treatment.

This blog is documentation of my path back to being a productive member of society. During the year 2013 I plan to improve my ability to deal with my anxiety and prepare myself to either go back to school or jump into a successful career (or both). Hopefully these blog posts will be of worth one day, either as the blueprint for a screenplay for a triumphant made-for-TV movie, or at least a cautionary tale about what not to do.
