Report: Day 12: No More Reports

Jan 13th, 2013 @ 4:49 AM

So, here’s the update… things have not been going well. I’ve basically spent a week in bed accomplishing almost nothing. This prospective freelance projects where I might get paid if things go well aren’t getting me out of bed. I stay awake until I fall asleep from exhaustion (sometime around five or six in the morning) and then I wake up when one of my parents gets sick of me sleeping all day (sometime around noon or two in the afternoon). In the last week I beat Portal and Portal 2 and watched four seasons of a television show. I feel terrible all the time.

Right now I need a job. I need structure. I need a place to go everyday and a time that I have to be there and something to accomplish and someone to check on me to make sure I’m accomplishing something. Also, getting paid would be nice. The main problem is that there’s currently no jobs that I’m “qualified” to fulfill. Here’s a breakdown:

Why I should be employable

  • I know the basics of many programming languages and technologies from low level embedded systems work up to web development and I’m pretty good at a couple of them.
  • I am a dynamite IT guy with proven experience pulling cables and lots of personal time spent fixing computers and setting up networks.
  • I am easily in the top 2% of the quickest learners I’ve ever met.
  • I am an intelligent, clean-cut, well spoken, white male. Seriously, isn’t getting a job supposed to be easy for me?

Why nobody wants to employ me

  • I have no degrees or certificates.
  • I’ve never had a programming job or completed a large enough programming project to prove to anyone that I can do it.
  • I am very bad at networking.

An ideal situation for me would be some kind of internship or apprenticeship where I would work on a team and be able to learn on the job. I would even be willing to work for a lower salary until I’m up to speed as my expenses are very low right now. Anyways, I’m starting to seriously look at jobs that are completely outside the computing world, but at least they’ll give me a paycheck and a place to be.
