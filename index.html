Report: Day 7

Jan 8th, 2013 @ 3:06 AM

Well, this reporting thing isn’t really working. Today sucked again. I got up on time to make it to my appointment a little bit late. I mentioned this blog and my psychologist didn’t think that it was necessarily a good idea. He made a good point about me creating the blog out of a need to feel in control of something which is not a motivation that leads to any sort of lasting change. I can think of some other reasons to keep it going, but I definitely can’t just sit around waiting for something to fix me. Anyways, it’s very possible that this blog will be taken offline after nine or ten posts only having been seen by me. I think it more likely that I will edit some of the existing posts and then post less often and only when I have something to say.

Reasons to keep this blog going

  • Reporting daily gives me a level of accountability.
  • I suck at consistently doing certain daily tasks.
  • The daily posts without worrying about being perfect have the potential to improve my abilities as a writer.
  • I think the daily data collection is useful for tracking certain things and I don’t think I will keep it up if I’m not posting it online.

I think it’s possible that my psychologist would have seen the blog differently if I had presented it more as a daily journal and less as a means of publicly publishing personal information. Anyways, I’ll keep thinking on it.

Report: Day 4

Jan 4th, 2013 @ 9:32 PM

Wow. Today was bad. I let the little things slide to the point where I had a fairly bad anxiety attack. I felt drawn to self-destructive behaviors. I wanted to drive WAY too fast and eat unhealthy food past the point of being full. I wanted to lay in bed and watch TV and play video games and surf the internet to try to stuff my mind with so much content that I would be able to forget about what a failure I’ve been.

The thing about feeling like this is I think I would be totally fine if I could just do everything in my life perfectly. If I could make every decision correctly the first time, I would never be awkward and I would never feel anxious. I could be confident and self-assured and 100% positive that I had done everything I could in exactly the right way. Being imperfect, however, means that I rethink and second-guess all my good decisions and my bad decisions devastate me.

Anyways, I found a free personal time punch clock program that should allow me to keep track of my time and help me be more productive. I was actually surprised that there wasn’t a better solution out there. I would be interested in a clean program with a nice interface (think github for windows) that would not only keep track of my time to various projects, but block certain time-wasters (facebook, reddit, um… iwastesomuchtime… etc.) while I’m “on the clock.” I might look into writing this sort of thing myself, but I need to focus on paying projects right now.

The day wasn’t a total loss, however. I came home from work to a nice dinner and Raiders of the Lost Ark on TV. Karen Allen is in all probability my long-lost, time-shifted soul mate. Once I invent time travel, I will save young her from her tedious and boring Hollywood life and introduce her to the glamour of being a programmer’s wife. Temple of Doom is on now. That’s a bit of a drag.
